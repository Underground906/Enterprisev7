# SECTION 2: CONTEXT DOC (For AI/Agents)

## System Overview

PIL_04_CONTENT provides **content strategy and research systems** for multi-platform publishing. The framework is built around solving audience pain points across 7 market segments (property-focused but universally applicable).

## Priority Audience Framework

### Tier 1: PRIMARY (Content-Hungry)
```
FIRST-TIME BUYERS
├── Pain Points: Deposit saving, mortgage confusion, area selection, hidden costs
├── Content Appetite: Extremely high - consume everything
├── Monetization: Newsletter upgrades, mortgage broker partnerships
└── Newsletter: "First-Time Buyer Friday"
```

### Tier 2: SECONDARY (High-Value)
```
BUY-TO-LET INVESTORS
├── Pain Points: Yield optimization, tax changes, tenant management
├── Content Appetite: Quality over quantity - want actionable insights
├── Monetization: Premium subscriptions, investment tools
└── Newsletter: "Investment Wednesday"
```

### Tier 3: TERTIARY (B2B)
```
PROPERTY PROFESSIONALS
├── Pain Points: Lead generation, market positioning, regulations
├── Content Appetite: Industry insights, networking, thought leadership
├── Monetization: Platform subscriptions, advertising
└── Newsletter: "Professional Tuesday"
```

## Daily Content Research Routine (30 min/day)

### MONDAY: Market Intelligence
```
SOURCES:
- Rightmove & Zoopla monthly reports
- ONS housing statistics
- Bank of England interest rate news
- Borough council planning applications
- Developer announcements

OUTPUTS:
- Weekly roundup articles
- Data visualizations
- Breaking news analysis
- Policy impact predictions

QUESTIONS:
- What surprised me?
- How does this affect my audiences?
- What's the human story?
- What would my audience ask?
```

### TUESDAY: Professional Pain Point Mining
```
SOURCES:
- LinkedIn property professional posts
- Estate agent Facebook groups
- Property industry forums
- Trade publication comments

GOLDMINES:
- "I wish someone had told me..." posts
- Complaint threads about regulations
- Success story celebrations
- Tool and technology discussions

QUESTIONS:
- What are they celebrating?
- What are they complaining about?
- What tools are they recommending?
- What changes worry them?
```

### WEDNESDAY: Investment & Landlord Research
```
SOURCES:
- Property investment Facebook groups
- Reddit: r/UKLandlords, r/UKPropertyInvestment
- Property Investors Network discussions
- SpareRoom/OpenRent landlord forums
- Property auction results

TRIGGERS:
- Yield calculations by area
- Tax change impacts
- Tenant stories
- Renovation ROI case studies
- New investment opportunities

VALIDATION:
- Would this help someone make money?
- Does this solve a costly problem?
- Is this actionable within 30 days?
- Can I provide concrete numbers?
```

### THURSDAY: Rental Market Deep Dive
```
SOURCES:
- Reddit: r/HousingUK, r/London
- SpareRoom forums
- Local Facebook renter groups
- Twitter rental discussions

FOCUS:
- Tenant experiences
- Rental trends
- Area recommendations
- Budget solutions
```

### FRIDAY: Consumer Questions
```
SOURCES:
- Google Trends
- Answer The Public
- Reddit questions
- Facebook group FAQs
- YouTube comments on property videos

FOCUS:
- What are people searching?
- What questions keep appearing?
- What fears need addressing?
- What celebrations can we amplify?
```

### WEEKEND: Idea Synthesis
```
PROCESS:
1. Review week's research notes
2. Identify top 10 content opportunities
3. Match to newsletter calendar slots
4. Draft headlines and angles
5. Schedule for production
```
